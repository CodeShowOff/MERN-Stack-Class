Spider-Man

Spider-Man, also known as Peter Parker, is one of the most beloved superheroes in comic book history. Created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko, Spider-Man first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962. What sets Spider-Man apart from other superheroes is his relatability; Peter Parker is an ordinary teenager dealing with everyday problems, from schoolwork to relationships, all while balancing his secret life as a superhero.

Gifted with incredible powers after being bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter gains the ability to cling to walls, superhuman strength, agility, and a "spider-sense" that warns him of danger. Using his scientific genius, he invents web-shooters that allow him to swing through the city and capture criminals. Despite his powers, Peter is constantly reminded that "with great power comes great responsibility," a lesson instilled in him by the tragic loss of his Uncle Ben.

Spider-Man's adventures often focus on his struggle to do the right thing, even when it comes at a personal cost. He faces a wide array of villains, from the Green Goblin to Doctor Octopus, but his greatest battles are often internal.
